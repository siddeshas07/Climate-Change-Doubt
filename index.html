What Have Others Done?

Perhaps the greatest success story of solving of climate change is the one by WWF, short for the World Wide Fund for Nature. WWF is a non-governmental organization whose main focus is to promote environmental conservation. Since its foundation in 1961, WWF is the primary driving force behind many of the environmental activism and conservation campaigns.

One of WWF's campaigns involves targeting politicians in control of the government, changing their mindset and pressuring them to act before climate change happens catastrophically. To do so, WWF ensured that the goal of keeping greenhouse gas levels within the limits is mentioned during the first international agreement on climate change. WWF has also started a program called Climate Savers in which it spearheaded the movement for large corporations to decrease their greenhouse gases emissions. This worked exceptionally well as the equivalent to Switzerland's annual emissions has been saved thanks to the program.

Apart from changing the political climate, the WWF has also made tremendous progress in the conservation of forests. It worked with multiple companies in the UK to ensure that their timber products come from well-managed forests, saving the ecosystem from unrecoverable damage and the lack of trees for CO2 absorption. To further regulate the timber market, the WWF formed the FSC, or the Forest Stewardship Council, to brand wooden products which meet sustainability standards.

Although us individuals are less powerful than the WWF when it comes to conservation activism, we can collectively request action from the government and corporations. Since the government and corporations rely heavily on everyday individuals for their success, they will be willing to change as long as we all stand together and demand so.

What Can We Do?

Lifestyle Changes

Changing our lifestyles is the most realistic method of solving climate change. Even though government incentives definitely do help accelerate the process, we as individuals have a lot of potential for driving progress.

The most impactful way to slow down climate change through driving down CO2 emissions is to reduce the number of children in a household. This goal is more relevant to developing countries since developed ones are already facing problems with the aging of the population. As countries develop, their citizens demand more resources in order to improve their lives. Compounded with the high fertility rate in developing countries, there is a lot of potential for an increase in CO2 emissions in the future.

Living a car free life is the second most effective lifestyle change we can make, although it may demand more policy action from the government to build infrastructure for mass transport. By living a car free life, cost of buying cars, car insurances, and maintainance may be eliminated. It is also important to consider that the government will be more incentivized to invest in public transport infrastructure if more of us decide to live a car free life. In the meantime, it is possible to drive down the effects of the use of automobiles through ride-share services like Uber and Lyft.

Keeping these considerations in mind, we can improve our lifestyle choices. However, it is also crucial to utilize our knowledge to convince others to switch their lifestyles.
